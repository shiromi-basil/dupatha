Activities to do in colombo

Colombo is the largest city in the always exciting island nation of Sri Lanka! Discover a side of the city that is miles apart from Colombo's 'commercial hub' status. Trips on 'tuk tuks', beach visits, shopping sprees, street food and more will ensure that your stay in the city is filled with fun experiences.

The list of fun things to do is in no particular order, and we hope it can give you some ideas of what to do when you visit!

Visit a temple

Spend some hours in spiritual reverence at the Gangaramaya Temple or the Bellanwila Temple. Don't forget to step into the museum at the Gangaramaya Temple to see unique sculptures and learn some interesting details about the temple and Sri Lanka in general.

Take in some art

The Colombo National Museum has some terrific examples of ancient paintings, sculptures and sketches dating back to the 4th century BC, while if it's something contemporary you want, the city has a thriving arts scene, with many creative painters exhibiting in local galleries.

Enjoy a city tour

Hop on board the exciting open deck double decker of the Colombo City Tour to witness the charm of Colombo.

Go for a ride in 'Tuk Tuk'

If you are in Colombo or in any other major town, one of the best ways to explore the city is to hop onto a three wheel or as the popularly known 'tuk tuk'. You can cover the whole town, get to know and experience all the major attractions and must visit places within a matter of hours as the 'tuk tuk' zig zags its way through the busy streets.

Watch the sunset at beach

Take some time from your busy schedule to visit either the Galle Face Green or the Mount Lavinia beach to enjoy some time by the ocean. If you happen to choose the Galle Face Green, make sure to fly a kite. The Mount Lavinia beach, similarly popular, is a good place to enjoy a spot of sea bathing, if the sea is not too rough, and then to some yummy seafood in one of the many.

Do Some Last Minute Shopping

One of Sri Lanka's best kept secrets is the shopping opportunities in Colombo -sleek department stores and cool shopping malls are filled with designer clothing, shoes and handbags, plus handicrafts, home furnishings and more.

Set sail on a swan pedal-boat

Lake South Beira is a nice place for an afternoon stroll, and its centre island is the number one destination for dates in Colombo–you'll spot couples cuddling under umbrellas everywhere. For more intimacy, your loved one and yourself can hire a swan-shaped pedal-boat and float away on the green waters–they are so opaque that ducks entirely disappear when they dive in to fish!

Try some street food

Try some street food from the many vendors scattered along the busy streets of Colombo or the Galle Face Green. Sri Lankan food is both tasty and filling. You’ll find kottu, fried rice, a host of random curries and a bunch of deep fried snacks. Try any of the random nameless stalls, or if you want to sit down while dining on Sri Lankan street food, head to a famous tented restaurant known as Nana Fast Food.

Restaurants

Colombo is a food heaven, a sensational culinary journey and indulgent experience that guarantees to tickle your taste buds, leaving you coming back for more. As the commercial hub of Sri Lanka, Colombo's dining scene reflects growing diversity, mixing exotic new restaurants with long standing ones. Value-for-money food courts, fine-dining restaurants and fast food outlets are only some of the limitless options.

Apart from the renowned Sri Lankan cuisine with its blend of coconut milk, onions, green chilli and aromatic spices like cinnamon and saffron, Colombo's international flavour is showcased by the wide variety of cuisines offered at our many restaurants, including, Chinese, Japanese, Thai, Indian, Italian, French, German and many more.
