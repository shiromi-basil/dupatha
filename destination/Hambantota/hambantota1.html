Hambantota town is often considered the heart of the “Deep South” of Sri Lanka. It’s a coastal town which has seen rapid development in recent years and promoted as a second commercial hub of the country after Colombo. The architecture from the Dutch and British periods are scattered throughout the district. The Hambantota area has long been known in Sri Lanka as the best source of salt in the land. It is also a convinient base to visit Tissamaharama and Kataragama.

Major Attractions

Mirijjawila Botanical Gardens

This is second largest botanical garden in Sri Lanka. Large variety of dry zone plants are scattered around. There are also a vriety of birds and butterflies in the garden.

Multi-Religious Place Of Worship

It is a site where you can see the buddhists, hindus' and muslims' place of worship side-by-side. It is located in kataragama an hour's drive from hambantota.

Mahinda Rajapaksa Port

The Hambantota Port is a maritime port in Sri Lanka. It is named after former President Mahinda Rajapaksa. It is built inland and operated by the SL Ports Authority.

Yala National Park

Beautiful wild life and the best national park to see a leopard also you can see so many other animals like mongoose, deers and crocodiles.

MRIC Stadium

It was built for the 2011 Cricket World Cup. The stadium has a capacity of 35,000 people making it the second largest stadium in SL.

Tissa Weva(Tissamahrama)

It is an artificial reservoir which was constructed in the 3 BC, in order to irrigate paddy lands and supply water to Tissamaharama.

Tissamaharama Raja Maha Viharaya

It was built by King Kavan Tissa in the 2nd Century BC. The Stupa is one of the largest dagabas in Sri Lanka.
