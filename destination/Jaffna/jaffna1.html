Inescapably, decades of war, emigration, embargoes and loss of life and property deeply affected this historic town, but the city is surprisingly green and leafy with beautiful temples and churches. Ancient sights both in the centre of town and on the outskirts make for compelling attractions. The city is an ideal base for forays to the idyllic islands just to the west and trips along the coastline and lagoons of the surrounding peninsula.

Major Attractions

Nallurn Kandaswamy Kovil

This huge Hindu temple, is one of the most significant Hindu religious complexes in Sri Lanka. Its sacred deity is Murugan.

Fort Jaffna

The Jaffna Fort was originally constructed by the Portuguese in 1618. A few years later it was captured by the Dutch and now only the ruins remain.

Jaffna Library

The library was built in 1933 and burnt in 1981. During the early 1980s, it was one of the biggest libraries in Asia and it was rebuilt in 2001.

Sangiliyan Statue

The Sangiliyan statue was dedicated to Cankili II a Tamil martyr and the last king of Jaffna Kingdom. The statue is a landmark of Jaffna

Casuarina Beach

The Casuarina Beach is the best beach in the Nothern Province and a go to for many travellers as it shallow and have smooth waves.

Jaffna Market

Jaffna city is famous for many things like Mango, Grape and Nelli Syrup.This is the go to place to get all jaffna products at a reasonable price.

Keerimalai Hot Springs

Keerimalai is also known as a natural pool which is said to have healing properties and there are separate spring for male and female.

Delft Island

Here you can see wild horses, Queen's Tower, Ruins of ancient Stupas and an old Portugese Fort but to get here it will take an hour by boat.

Clock Tower

Clock Tower was erected in 1875 to honour a visit by the Prince of Wales.The clock were replaced in 2000 by Prince Charles.
