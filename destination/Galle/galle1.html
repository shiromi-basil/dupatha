Galle is a jewel. A Unesco World Heritage Site this historic city is a delight to explore on foot, an endlessly exotic old trading port blessed with imposing Dutch-colonial buildings, ancient mosques and churches, grand mansions and museums. Wandering its rambling lanes you'll pass stylish cafes, quirky boutiques and impeccably restored hotels owned by local and foreign artists, writers, photographers and designers.

Major Attractions

Dutch Reformed Church

The Dutch Reformed Church, is also known as Groote Kerk, is one of the oldest Protestant churches in Sri Lanka. It was built in 1755.

Galle Lighthouse

The Galle Lighthouse was built in 1939. The light station is within the walls of the ancient Galle Fort, a UNESCO world heritage site.

Gingota Silk Factory

Only place in Sri Lanka to witness an informative walk tour for the making of silk from the mulberry plantation to the final weaving.

Fort Meeran Mosque

This mosque is around 300 years old although it was refurbished 120 years ago, is the biggest and best known mosque in Galle

Dutch Fort

It is one of the most important historic monuments to illustrate the European influence in South East Asia during their rule.

National Maritime Museum

This museum showcases a collection of boat models, maps and things found from ship-wrecks, some almost a century old.

Whale Watching

In Mirissa, you can spot whales, dolphins and sea turtles. The best time to whale watch is during November-April.

Unwatuna Bay

It is one of the cleanest beaches and it is in the Top 100 list of beaches.It is popular for spear fishing, snorkeling and surfing.

Flag Rock

Flag Rock, at the southernmost end of the Fort, was once a Portuguese bastion.It is the most popular place to catch a sunset.
