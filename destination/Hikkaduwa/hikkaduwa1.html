There are plenty of charming places to stay, a string of mellow beach bars and restaurants, good diving and surf schools and plenty of interesting excursions accessible by three-wheeler or rental scooter. For years there has been talk about a possible bypass road around Hikkaduwa, which if it were to materialise would do wonders for this paradise lost.

Major Attractions

Hikkaduwa Beach

Turquoise blue ocean holds ample sealife including sea turtles and a variety of fish and it is the best surfing spots in Sri Lanka.

Seenigama Vihara

The Seenigama Temple is located on a very small island just off the coast at Hikkaduwa. This is the temple of God 'Dewol'.

Tsunami Hoganji Vihara

Donated by the Japanese Government following the 2004 Tsunami it represents the height of the wave that killed the passengers on the train.

Narigama Beach

Narigama beach is long and wide with glorious soft yellow sand and clear waters. It is located just south of Hikkaduwa Beach.

Turtle Hatchery Hikkaduwa

It's a place where a small variety of turtles are reared and taken care of. If you are an animal lover do check the place out.

Madu Ganga

This is a popular place to take a boat safari, where one can see beautiful mangroves,and colourful birds including herons and kingfishers

Gangarama Temple

This interesting Buddhist temple has lots of popular educational paintings that are the work of one man over nearly a decade.

Hikkaduwa Coral Gardens

Go snorkelling to witness the beautiful variety of colourful fish and corals.An amazing experience you don't want to miss!
