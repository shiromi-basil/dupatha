Kings ruled the central plains of Sri Lanka from Polonnaruwa 800 years ago, when it was a thriving commercial and religious centre. The glories of that age can be found in the archaeological treasures that still give a pretty good idea of how the city looked in its heyday. You'll find the archaeological park a delight to explore, with hundreds of ancient structures – tombs and temples, statues and stupas – in a compact core. The Quadrangle alone is worth the trip. That Polonnaruwa is close to elephant-packed national parks only adds to its popularity.

Major Attractions

Minneriya National Park

It is the largest known meeting place of Asian Elephants in the world. The vegetation of the park consists of tropical plants, grasslands and wetlands.

Gal Vihara

A Buddhist Temple at Polonnaruwa has the most perfect specimen of Buddha statue hewn out of solid stone. Crafted in 1153 A.C.

Parakrama Samudra

This Vewa was built by King Parakramabahu and today only a portion of his creation exists as it originally consisted of five large reservoirs

Polonnaruwa Vatadage

This beautiful Stupa House has been built before the time of Nissanga Malla (1187-1196) and there are beautifully carved Buddha statues in it.

Hatadage

It is another Sacred tooth reliquary which was built by King Nissankamalla. The inscriptions therein record the achievements of him.

Royal Palace

It was constructed by Parakramabahu I, and is said to have had seven storeys. The roof in this main hall which had 50 rooms in total.

Nissanka Latha Mandapaya

It is a square building and the stone pillars are carved in the shape of a lotus stork and are curved in three places to make it a lotus.

Lankatilaka Vihara

The Buddha statue in the Temple is completely made out of clay bricks and its height is 41 feet. It was constructed by King Parakramabahu

Gal Potha

This Stone Book is one of the works of King Nissanka which describes himself, his rule and the eligibility for being a king of Sri Lanka
