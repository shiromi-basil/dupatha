Golden Rules of Travel

Sri Lanka is a land which takes pride in its natural beauty. Even with its cities and skycrapers it is well stocked with forrests and beaches. When you travel in Sri Lanka the production of garbage is innevitable.

DO NOT LITTER. Take ALL Your Garbage Home

Keep a shopping bag with you and put ALL your litter (including bio-degradable garbage) in it.At the end of the day, dispose of it in your guest house, or take it home. Do not leave YOUR garbage all along the road, where you had a picnic and bath.Leave it ONLY in your garbage bag, or in YOUR pocket.

Make sure you have access to maps while travelling. But if you find yourself lost anywhere do not hesistate to ask a local. You will find yourself suurounded by people who would love to give you directions.

You should always prepare where you will be making stops when you plan to travel for long hours. Public restrooms are scarce outside the city, and even if you find one it may not be in great shape. Prepare to make stops at restaurants or local buisnesses for a comfortable journey

Do not smoke or consume liquor in PUBLIC SPACES

If you cannot do without alcohol and smoking, do it privately in your guest house or room. NOT in public places. CONSUMING ALCOHOL AND SMOKING IN PUBLIC PLACES IS PUNISHABLE BY LAW. Try to stay out of trouble to enjoy your stay here

Be aware of places which allow and do not allow photography. There may be an important reason why photography may not be allowed in a specific area, and if you do not abide by these rules your equipment could get confiscated by local authorities.



On Safari

If everyone takes home a plant, a flower a fruit.what will be left in the forest? Take only memories and photos. Do not speed in vehicles, do not drive off the trail.all this destroys fragile nature Keep quiet, listen to the sounds of nature.It is what you came here for.

At a Wewa or Stream be respectful towards villagers that use the water. Be mindful of animals that use the water. Be sensible and consider your own safety. Check with locals regarding particular dangers of the location At Archaeological Sites

Do not scratch your name on anything.Avoid standing on ancient walls, pushing and moving ancient stones and bricks Be respectful if photographing people near shrines and statues, even if they are ruins if there are resident Bhikkus, get permission from the head priest before proceeding In functioning monasteries please visit at allowed times, do not disturb the meditating monks.
